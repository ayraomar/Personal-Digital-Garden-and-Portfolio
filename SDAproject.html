♡ Software Design and Analysis Semester Project ♡

Our project is to select a real-world system, without any limitations in terms of skill or personal aptitude as we are not required to write the code, only to design the system.

Here's where I will be documenting my work on the assigned project. We're due to start on the second phase of the project soon. Looking forward to share the details soon!

Stay tuned to see how it goes <3

Phase 1

To begin our project, we had to make an SRS document. This meant that we had to introduce our problem, propose a solution, and explain where it would be relevant.

Additionally, we were required to submit a Context Diagram and a Use-Case Diagram to support our solution.

I'm working on this project in a group of 4, so we divided this work amongst outselves based on preference and first-come first-serve.

We unanimously decided to choose the problem that comes with research. With the rise of academic publications, it's difficult to find exactly what it is you're looking for. Plus, the jargon and needlessly complicated wording is discouraging to aspiring researchers and students, preventing them from reaping the benefits of academic works.

Introducing the problem and explaining it further was my job.

My group member, Duaa, took on explaining our proposed solution. We decided that a Research Copilot would be an optimal solution to our problem. This digital assistant aims to provide users with a dynamic space that pulls out the information you need from an article without you needing to go through it and manually dissect it or beg it to give you the answers it seems to so closely guard. It also has the functionality of finding contradictory and complimentary research in order to provide plenty of sources. Finally, there's a built-in chatbot that can explain concepts, answer questions, and simplify complicated terminology. All in all, Duaa did an excellent job detailing the solution we'd come up with.

Asra worked on defining the scope of the project. She listed pre-existing solutions, the inclusions, and the exclusions.

Finally, Ruhab explained the objectives and provided the Context and Use-Case Diagrams for our model.
